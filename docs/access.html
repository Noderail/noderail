Early Access

Your intellectual work deserves a permanent home.

NodeRail is in early access. We are building the first cohort of researchers, students, and practitioners who will publish the platform's founding nodes. Tell us about your work and we will be in touch within 48 hours.

Who we are inviting to join

NodeRail is for anyone with intellectual work that deserves to persist, compound, and be cited.

Researchers PhD Students Postdocs Practitioners Field Founders Universities Research Centres Policy Organisations

Submit your work

Takes under five minutes. We respond within 48 hours.

Be specific. This is the most important part of your application. The more clearly you describe your work, the better we can support you.

What happens after you submit

Your submission is assessed

Every submission is reviewed against the criteria below. We respond within 48 hours with a clear next step.

A brief onboarding conversation

For most applicants, we schedule a 20-minute call to understand your work and help you structure your first node correctly.

You publish your first node

We walk you through the node template. Your first structured, citable piece of intellectual work goes live on NodeRail.

Your DOI is issued

On your first stable release, Zenodo issues a permanent DOI. Your work is citable in academic papers and reports from that point forward.

What we assess submissions for

Submissions are assessed for fit with the NodeRail model, not prestige or institutional affiliation. The question is whether your work is the kind of intellectual work NodeRail is designed to support.

Structured intellectual work A framework, model, measurement instrument, research project, or field definition — not a blog post or general opinion.
Continuity intent You want your work to persist, be built upon, and be cited — not just shared once and forgotten.
Willingness to use the node template NodeRail works because nodes follow a consistent structure. You need to be willing to structure your work using the provided templates.
Open to contribution NodeRail is a shared infrastructure. Work published here is open for others to fork, extend, and build upon — with full attribution.
Institutional affiliation is not required Independent researchers, practitioners, and students without institutional backing are equally welcome.

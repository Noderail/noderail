# Emotional Residue

**Construct Type:** Measurement Node  
**Field:** Human Capacity Science  
**Version:** v0.1.0 · Draft  
**Evidence Level:** Practitioner-Derived  
**Permitted Use:** Open Exploration  

---

## Definition

The unprocessed emotional weight carried forward from one event, interaction, or task into the next. When feelings from a previous experience have not been acknowledged or resolved, they occupy cognitive and emotional bandwidth in subsequent contexts — reducing available capacity without the individual being aware of the source.

---

## Why This Construct Matters

Most performance models treat emotions as independent of task state. Emotional Residue challenges this assumption: unresolved emotional content from a previous context does not disappear when the context changes. It persists as background processing load, consuming bandwidth that would otherwise be available for thinking, deciding, and creating.

The individual may experience this as difficulty concentrating, unexpected irritability, low motivation, or a general sense of heaviness — without connecting these states to the earlier event. This invisibility is precisely what makes Emotional Residue a measurement challenge and a design opportunity.

---

## Construct Dimensions

| Dimension | Description |
|---|---|
| **Intensity** | The strength of the unresolved emotional charge |
| **Recency** | How recently the triggering event occurred |
| **Source awareness** | Whether the individual can identify the source of the residue |
| **Carry duration** | How long the residue has been active across contexts |
| **Impact on output** | Observable or self-reported effects on capacity and performance |

---

## Measurement Approach (v0.1)

### Method: Self-Report Pulse (Privacy-First)

Emotional Residue at v0.1 is measured through brief, consent-based self-report prompts designed to surface residue without requiring disclosure of the triggering content.

**Sample probe items (5-point scale: Not at all → Completely):**
- "Something from an earlier interaction is still sitting with me."
- "I'm carrying weight from something that happened before this task."
- "My mind keeps returning to a recent event even when I try to focus."
- "I feel less available than usual for the work in front of me — and I'm not sure why."

**Scoring:**
- **0–1:** No detectable residue
- **2:** Mild residue — may not affect output
- **3:** Moderate residue — likely narrowing capacity window
- **4–5:** High residue — significant bandwidth reduction; reset intervention indicated

### Complementary Signals (Non-Invasive)
- Self-reported task entry difficulty
- Voluntary context-switching frequency
- Pre-task readiness rating

## Relationship to Other HCS Constructs

| Construct | Relationship |
|---|---|
| **Cognitive Load** | Emotional Residue increases effective cognitive load even without additional task demands |
| **Digital Fatigue** | Digital Fatigue and Emotional Residue compound each other; both deplete the same finite bandwidth pool |
| **Capacity Windows** | Unresolved residue is a primary driver of Narrow and Closed capacity windows |
| **Human Bandwidth Index (HBI)** | Emotional Residue is a core pillar of HBI scoring |
| **Capacity-Relative Time** | High residue renders a given time block functionally shorter than its clock duration |

---

## Intervention Signals

When Emotional Residue is detected at moderate to high levels, the following reset categories are indicated:

- **Micro reset:** Brief acknowledgment practice (naming the emotion without elaborating)
- **Meso reset:** Transitional buffer between contexts (walk, silence, unstructured time)
- **Macro reset:** Structured debrief or processing time within the work design

> Note: The goal is not to eliminate emotion from work — it is to prevent unacknowledged emotion from silently consuming capacity.

---

## Ethics and Safeguards

- Probe items must never require disclosure of the emotional content itself
- Data is owned by the individual, not the organisation
- Scores must never be used for performance ranking or discipline
- Aggregated team-level data (anonymised) may inform workload design, not individual monitoring
- Individuals must be able to skip residue measurement at any time without penalty

## Validity Notes (v0.1)

This construct is practitioner-derived, grounded in 108+ customer discovery interviews conducted by the field founder. It draws on adjacent constructs in cognitive psychology (attention residue, ego depletion, affect-as-information) but has not yet undergone formal empirical validation as a standalone HCS measurement.

**Evidence base:** Practitioner observation, qualitative interview synthesis, adjacent peer-reviewed literature  
**Validation status:** Pre-empirical (v0.1 roadmap includes pilot validation study design)

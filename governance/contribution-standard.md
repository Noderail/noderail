## THE FOUNDING STATEMENT

**NodeRail is governed knowledge infrastructure for the AI era.**

For too long, knowledge has belonged to institutions — not to the people who build it. The researcher without a .edu address. The student whose six months of work earned a grade and then disappeared. The elder whose oral tradition has no infrastructure. The founder whose field doesn't have a home yet.

NodeRail changes that.

We provide the trust layer that knowledge has always needed — provenance, attribution, versioning, and governance — so that original thinking can be seen, cited, built upon, and protected, on the contributor's terms.

No gatekeepers. No disappearing into a desktop. No waiting for permission to matter.

---

## WHO NODERAIL IS FOR

NodeRail is for anyone with original thinking who wants their knowledge governed, protected, and citable — regardless of where they come from or what institutions have recognised them.

This includes the researcher at a leading university and the researcher with no institutional affiliation. The student whose project earned a grade and the professor whose paper has a DOI. The elder whose oral tradition has no infrastructure and the archivist working inside one. The founder whose field doesn't have a home yet and the established academic extending an existing one.

NodeRail does not privilege institutional contributors over independent ones — or independent over institutional. It applies the same standard of provenance, attribution, and governance to everyone.

NodeRail was built with particular intentionality for those who have been kept outside existing systems — but its doors are open to all who come with original thinking and honest evidence.

## THE CORE PRINCIPLE

*NodeRail's role is to serve your knowledge — not to replace it. Whatever mode you choose, what leaves NodeRail must still be recognisably, verifiably yours.*

## THE THREE CONTRIBUTION MODES

Before submitting, every contributor chooses their mode. This is not a technical setting. It is a contract between the contributor and the system.

---

### PRESERVE
*Format only. Zero new content.*

The system structures your work, adds frontmatter, and makes it machine-readable and citable. Your words are your words. Nothing is added, reordered, or reframed. What goes in comes out — governed and citable.

**The system can:** add frontmatter, fix markdown structure, generate citation format
**The system cannot:** add new content, reframe existing content, introduce new dimensions or examples

*Use when your work is complete and you want it protected exactly as it is.*

---

### RECONSTRUCT
*Clarify only. No new information.*

The system can reorganise and sharpen what is already there — making your meaning clearer, removing redundancy, improving structure. It cannot add dimensions, examples, frameworks, or ideas you did not originate. Everything in the node came from you.

**The system can:** reorganise structure, improve clarity, remove redundancy, sharpen language
**The system cannot:** introduce new information, add measurement dimensions, create probe items, write roadmap items you did not originate

*Use when your thinking is solid but needs structural clarity before it is citable.*

---

### FORGE
*Human-in-the-loop. You originate. The system shapes. You approve.*

A governed back-and-forth session where you bring raw thinking and the system asks questions, reflects back, and proposes structure. Nothing enters the node without your explicit approval at each step. The session ends with a fully attributed, citable node — built from your thinking, shaped collaboratively, verified by you.

**The system can:** ask questions, reflect your words back, propose structure, suggest dimensions based on what you have said
**The system cannot:** introduce content you have not originated or approved, move to the next section without your sign-off

**How a Forge session works:**
1. Contributor brings a raw idea, concept, or working definition
2. System asks: *"Tell me more about what you mean by..."*
3. Contributor responds in their own words
4. System reflects back: *"Here is what I heard — is this accurate?"*
5. Contributor approves, corrects, or expands
6. Repeat until the node is complete
7. Final node presented for full review before publication

*Use when your idea is still forming and needs a structured process to become a governed, citable node.*

## THE CONTRIBUTOR STANDARD

### What NodeRail guarantees every contributor

- Your name is on your work. Always.
- Your original words are preserved. In Preserve and Reconstruct modes, nothing is added without your initiation. In Forge mode, nothing is added without your approval.
- Your work is timestamped. The date of contribution is recorded and immutable — establishing when your knowledge entered the record.
- Your work receives a DOI. Every published node is assigned a permanent digital object identifier — making it citable in academic, professional, and institutional contexts.
- Your evidence level is declared honestly. NodeRail does not inflate the status of your work. Practitioner-derived is practitioner-derived. Pre-empirical is pre-empirical. The system will never present your work as more validated than it is.
- Your work is versioned. Every change is tracked. The original is always recoverable.
- You control access. You decide what is open, what is restricted, and what requires community consent.

### What NodeRail will never do

- Publish a node without contributor review and approval
- Introduce content the contributor did not originate
- Misrepresent evidence level
- Allow contributed knowledge to be extracted without attribution
- Use capacity data or contribution data for ranking, surveillance, or commercial exploitation without consent

## INDIGENOUS AND CULTURAL KNOWLEDGE — COMMUNITY SOVEREIGNTY

For indigenous and cultural knowledge, an additional protection layer applies.

**Community Sovereignty** means the originating community — not only the individual contributor — holds governance rights over the knowledge. No node containing indigenous or cultural knowledge can be published, accessed, or built upon without community-defined consent protocols.

This means:
- The contributing elder or culture bearer defines who can access the node
- The community of origin is named in the node and in every citation
- Commercial use requires explicit community consent — not just individual contributor consent
- NodeRail will not override community governance decisions

*This layer exists because indigenous knowledge has been extracted, misattributed, and commercialised without consent for centuries. NodeRail will not participate in that history.*

---

## THE COLLABORATION LAYER

NodeRail is not only for individual contributors. Knowledge grows when people build on each other's work — with full attribution to every origin point.

When someone builds on your node:
- The relationship is recorded in the lineage
- Your original node remains unchanged
- The new node declares its parent
- You are visible in every downstream citation

This is **traceable collaboration** — not open-source free-for-all. Every contribution, every fork, every extension — the full genealogy of an idea is always visible.

---

## NODERAIT IN ONE SENTENCE

*NodeRail makes sure that when you produce knowledge — wherever you come from, whoever you are — it can be seen, cited, traced back to you, and built upon. On your terms.*

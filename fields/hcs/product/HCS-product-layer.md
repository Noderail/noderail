## HBI Quiz — The Front Door

### Structure
Three signal pillars → 12–15 questions → HBI score → Capacity Zone

**Signal 1: Cognitive Load (4 questions)**
- Mental task switching frequency
- Open tasks / unfinished loops
- Decision fatigue level
- Ability to pick a clear next step

**Signal 2: Digital Fatigue (4 questions)**
- Tab/notification load
- Re-entry friction after interruption
- Focus stability
- Hours of sustained digital engagement

**Signal 3: Emotional Residue (4 questions — REVISED)**
- "Right now, are you carrying anything outside of work that is taking up mental or emotional space?" (captures life → work carryover)
- "Do you find your mind returning to a personal issue, worry, or unresolved situation during the day?"
- "After a stressful interaction or personal moment, how long does it usually stay with you?"
- "Does something in your personal life currently make it harder to focus, decide, or recover mentally?"

**Optional (Open text):**
- "Is there anything currently affecting your capacity that you wish tools understood better?"

### Scoring
- Green: 75–100 — Capacity available
- Yellow: 50–74 — Capacity compressed
- Red: <50 — Capacity depleted

### Quiz Lead Message
*"This quiz gives you a snapshot of your Human Bandwidth — your available capacity to think, decide, and execute right now. Takes 3 minutes."*

## The Three Capacity Profiles

### GREEN — Full Tank

**Core message:** This is your power window. Use it for what matters most — not what's loudest.

**What this means:**
- Emotional residue is low or processed
- Cognitive loops are relatively closed
- Digital environment is manageable
- Recovery is happening

**Permission slip:**
You don't need to earn this state. You've cleared enough load for capacity to come through. The window is open. Use it intentionally.

**Top priorities in this state:**
1. Deep work on your most important task
2. Strategic decisions you've been deferring
3. Creative work requiring full bandwidth
4. Conversations requiring your full presence

**Reset Recipes:**
- Flow Lane Lock (protect this window)
- Shutdown Seal (end the day properly to preserve tomorrow)

**Watch for:** The temptation to fill the window with urgent-but-unimportant tasks. Protect this capacity.

---

### YELLOW — Half Tank

**Core message:** You don't have a discipline problem. You have a capacity timing problem.

**What this means:**
- Emotional residue is present and active
- Cognitive load is elevated — too many open loops
- Digital friction is adding compound cost
- Recovery is insufficient or blocked

**What this is NOT:**
Not laziness. Not incompetence. Not weakness. It's a state — and states are temporary.

**Immediate priority:**
Reduce switching before pushing. Reset first. Decide second.

**Top resets for Yellow:**
1. Tab Tamer (close the open loops)
2. Open Loop Dump (get what's running in the background onto paper)
3. Meeting Detox (process the emotional residue from the last call)
4. Re-entry Ramp (get back in without crashing)

**24-hour rule:** No major decisions today without a reset first. If you must decide, do it after a Tab Tamer + 10-minute break.

**7-day plan:**
- Day 1–2: Triage open loops
- Day 3–4: Protect at least one 90-min uninterrupted block
- Day 5–7: Build a shutdown ritual to close the day properly

**Watch for:** Rising tab chaos. Difficulty choosing the next step. Rereading the same thing multiple times. These are signals to reset — not push.

---

### RED — Empty Tank

**Core message:** Stop pushing. You're making tomorrow worse.

**What this means:**
- Capacity is depleted across multiple dimensions simultaneously
- Emotional residue is high and active
- Cognitive load has exceeded available bandwidth
- Recovery is absent or severely insufficient
- The Doom Loop is active

**What this is NOT:**
Not a character flaw. Not a performance issue. Not a permanent state.

**Right now:**
This is not the moment to prove yourself. This is the moment to protect yourself.

**For the next 24 hours:**
- No major decisions
- No unnecessary meetings
- No multitasking
- No forcing focus through exhaustion
- One task. Do it. Stop.

**Immediate protocol:**
1. Overload Override (2–4 min)
2. Write one sentence: what actually needs to happen today (not everything — one thing)
3. Do that one thing. Nothing else.
4. Legend Recovery before end of day.

**What to say to yourself:**
*"I am not failing. I am overloaded. The goal today is not excellence. The goal is to stop making tomorrow worse."*

**Warning:** Continuing to push at Red state is not discipline. It is damage. It compounds capacity debt and delays recovery.

## The Reset Library

### Philosophy
Resets are not productivity hacks. They are capacity restoration protocols — micro-interventions that interrupt the Doom Loop before crash.

Reset first. Decide second.

### Full Library

| Reset | Duration | Use When | Mode |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Tab Tamer** | 45–60s | Tab chaos, can't see priorities | Flow |
| **Flow Lane Lock** | 2–3 min | Constant switching, can't drop in | Flow |
| **Re-entry Ramp** | 60–120s | Interrupted, can't restart | Flow |
| **Meeting Detox** | 90s | Post-call fog, emotional hangover | Zen |
| **Open Loop Dump** | 30–60s | Mind rehearsing unfinished things | Zen/Flow |
| **Context Switch Cleanse** | 2–3 min | Task transitions feel expensive | Flow |
| **Overload Override** | 2–4 min | Urgency spike, doom-scroll pull | Zen |
| **Digital Friction Insert** | 30–90s | Compulsive checking, autopilot | Zen |
| **Legend Recovery** | 3–6 min | Pushed hard, feeling brittle | Legend |
| **Shutdown Seal** | 2–5 min | End-of-day buzzing brain | Zen/Legend |
| **Interaction Reset** | 3 min | Conversation replaying in your mind | Zen |

### Three Modes

**Zen Mode** — Reduce input, stabilize the system
*Use when:* noise, agitation, overstimulation, anxiety, doom-scroll pull

**Flow Mode** — Protect the attention lane
*Use when:* fragmented attention, constant switching, can't start, re-entry friction

**Legend Mode** — Controlled high output + mandatory recovery
*Use when:* pushing hard on important work, sprint needed, post-sprint recovery

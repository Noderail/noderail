# Human Capacity Science (HCS)

## One-Line Definition

Human Capacity Science is the study and measurement of how human capacity fluctuates in real time, and how systems can be designed to protect, restore, and optimize that capacity.

## Field Statement

Human Capacity Science (HCS) is an emerging interdisciplinary field concerned with the real-time variability of human capacity and the conditions that influence it. While existing fields examine cognitive load, emotional regulation, and digital interaction independently, HCS proposes an integrated model that studies how these dimensions interact dynamically — and how systems can be designed to preserve and restore capacity.

**The core claim:** Performance failure is often a capacity timing problem, not a knowledge problem.

## What HCS Is NOT

HCS is not:
- Productivity optimization
- Generic wellness or burnout recovery
- Biohacking
- A rebrand of cognitive load theory alone
- Digital wellbeing dashboards
- Clinical diagnosis

HCS is:
- A measurement science
- A systems design discipline
- A human infrastructure layer
- An integrative real-time capacity model

## Core Premise

> "Performance is not primarily a function of skill or knowledge. It is a function of available capacity at a given moment."

The reframe:
- Not: *"Can this person do the work?"*
- But: *"Do they have the capacity to access what they know right now?"*

## Four Core Principles

**1. Capacity is Not Stable**
Capacity fluctuates within individuals more than between individuals. Same person, same skill → different outcomes depending on capacity state.

**2. Capacity is Multi-Dimensional**
Capacity is the result of interacting load systems: Emotional, Cognitive, Digital.

**3. Capacity is a System Condition**
HCS extends beyond the individual. Capacity is shaped by workflows, tools, policies, and social environments. Systems don't just use capacity — they shape and degrade it.

**4. Capacity is the Primary Constraint**
*"We measure everything except the human operating the system."*

## The Human Capacity Equation (Core Law)

```
Usable Capacity(t) = Potential Capacity − Capacity Leakage(t) + Capacity Recovery(t)
```

Where:
- **Potential Capacity** = maximum capacity under ideal conditions
- **Capacity Leakage** = capacity lost to system pressures and friction
- **Capacity Recovery** = restoration through rest, closure, transition
- **t** = time (capacity is dynamic, not fixed)

## Three Pillars

**Pillar 1 — Capacity Modeling**
How capacity behaves: cognitive, emotional, digital load dynamics, mechanisms, curves, thresholds.

**Pillar 2 — Capacity Measurement**
Behavioral signals, digital traces, computational models. The HBI (Human Bandwidth Index) is the primary measurement construct.

**Pillar 3 — Capacity Optimization**
Interventions, system design, recovery conditions, technologies that modulate and restore capacity.

## Research Grounding

HCS integrates findings from:
- Cognitive Load Theory (Sweller)
- Attention Residue (Sophie Leroy)
- Emotional Labor (Hochschild)
- Technostress (Tarafdar et al.)
- Psychological Detachment / Recovery (Sonnentag)
- Intraindividual Variability research
- Attention Span research (Gloria Mark)
- Executive Function and Working Memory models (Baddeley, TBRS)
- Affective Neuroscience
- Neuroergonomics
